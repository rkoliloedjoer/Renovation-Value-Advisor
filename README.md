# Renovation-Value-Advisor

Final project for the Building AI course

## Summary

This AI tool analyzes property listings, local neighborhood trends, and regulations to predict the renovations that will maximize resale value. It provides cost estimates, timelines, and expected value uplift, helping homeowners invest smartly.

Building AI course project


## Background

Many homeowners renovate based on personal taste rather than resale value, leading to emotional decisions that don’t maximize return. It’s often unclear which upgrades yield profit. People also lack localized data—what boosts value in one neighborhood might not in another. This tool helps both homebuyers and investors make data-driven renovation choices. Additionally, it can reveal hidden upside, helping buyers confidently bid, knowing future value potential.

## How is it used?

Users:
This tool is for homebuyers and real estate investors who want to maximize resale value. It’s used during the property search or pre-purchase planning phase.

Uploaded Information:
Users provide property details—such as images (kitchen, exterior, etc.), descriptions, location—and their renovation budget.

AI Process:
The AI evaluates current property conditions, compares them to local market trends, and checks regulatory factors. It identifies the most valuable renovations and their complexity.

Output:
The tool provides prioritized renovation recommendations, cost estimates, projected value uplift, and even how much additional bidding confidence that uplift can offer.


## Data sources and AI methods

We use public real estate listings for initial property data. Once a buyer shows interest, agents may provide blueprints or exact plot details. Local government planning data and market trends help refine insights. Users can also upload their own site-visit photos. 

For AI methods, we apply image analysis to property features, predictive modeling for value uplift, and natural language processing for regulations and listing descriptions. This combination powers the tailored recommendations.

## Challenges

Data sourcing: Public real estate listings may not allow scraping, so legal permissions must be confirmed.
No guarantees: The tool provides predictions but cannot guarantee resale success or bidding outcomes.
Budget and timeline risks: Actual renovation costs or timelines could exceed estimates, requiring clear disclaimers.
Market volatility: Predictions rely on historical trends and may not foresee future market shifts.
Personal taste: Users’ personal preferences could still influence decisions beyond resale value.

## What next?

The project could expand by incorporating more real-time market data and partnering with real estate platforms to ensure data compliance. 
It could also integrate construction cost databases for more accurate budgeting. To grow, we’d need real estate domain experts, AI engineers specializing in computer vision and NLP, and legal advisors to navigate data usage rights.
